The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) is a four-year degree program that prepares students for a career in the rapidly growing field of information technology. The program covers a wide range of topics, including computer hardware and software, programming, database management, networking, and information systems. Students also learn about the ethical and social implications of information technology. BSIT graduates are qualified for a variety of jobs in the IT industry, such as software developer, web developer, database administrator, network administrator, and systems analyst. They can also work as IT consultants, trainers, or technical support specialists. The BSIT program is a good choice for students who are interested in a career in IT and who want to learn the skills and knowledge they need to be successful in this field. The program is also a good choice for students who are interested in starting their own IT business.

The Associate in Computer Technology (ACT) is a two-year degree program that provides students with the skills and knowledge they need to start a career in information technology. The program covers a variety of topics, including computer programming, data structures and algorithms, computer architecture, database management, and networking. Students also learn about the ethical and social implications of information technology. ACT graduates are qualified for a variety of entry-level jobs in the IT industry, such as web developer, software tester, junior network administrator, and technical support specialist. They can also work as IT help desk technicians or computer operators. The ACT program is a good choice for students who are interested in a career in IT and who want to learn the skills and knowledge they need to be successful in this field in a relatively short amount of time. The program is also a good choice for students who are not sure if they want to commit to a four-year degree program.
